\chapter{Comments on the Skeleton}


Again, the thesis is a formal document designed to address the examiner's
two main questions. Section~\ref{sec:StateoftheArt} and Chapter~\ref{sec:ResearchQ} show that you have chosen a good problem, and section~\ref{sec:Steps} shows that you solved it. Chapters~\ref{sec:intro} and ~\ref{sec:back} lead the reader into the problem, and section~\ref{sec:conc} highlights the main knowledge generated by the whole exercise.

Note also that everything that others did is carefully separated from
everything that you did. Knowing who did what is important to the
examiners. Chapter~\ref{sec:ResearchQ}, the problem statement, is the obvious dividing line. That's the main reason for putting it in the middle in this formal
document.
